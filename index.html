About

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer and Information Science at the University of Michigan Rackham Graduate School, specializing in Systems and Security. My research focuses on trustworthy machine learning, with emphasis on defending models against data poisoning and backdoor attacks. I study how training samples influence model parameter updates and develop systems to detect malicious behavior in training data.

Featured Research

PoisonSpot

PoisonSpot is a defense system that protects machine learning models from stealthy clean-label data poisoning attacks by tracing training-sample influence on model parameter updates.

The system outperforms the state-of-the-art defense ASSET on benchmark datasets including CIFAR-10, STL-10, and ImageNet-100.

The framework was released as an open-source artifact and received ACM CCS 2025 badges for artifact availability, functionality, and reproduced results.
